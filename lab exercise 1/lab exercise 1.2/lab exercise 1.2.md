This circuit demonstrates an Arduino Uno controlling 11 LEDs connected in parallel through individual resistors. Each LED is attached to a separate digital output pin of the Arduino, allowing the microcontroller to control the LEDs independently. The resistors are used to limit the current flowing through each LED to prevent damage and ensure stable operation. The circuit is powered through the Arduino, while the breadboard helps organize the ground and power connections neatly.

The main feature of this project is the lighting sequence created by the LEDs. The LEDs are programmed to turn on from both ends of the row at the same time, moving step by step toward the center. As the sequence progresses, the illuminated LEDs appear to “meet” in the middle, creating a visually appealing synchronization effect. This pattern gives the impression of motion and symmetry, making the circuit useful for learning animation techniques in Arduino programming.

After the LEDs meet at the center, the sequence reverses direction. The LEDs then light outward from the middle toward the ends, producing the opposite effect. This continuous inward-and-outward movement creates a smooth and repetitive lighting animation. The project demonstrates important concepts such as digital output control, timing delays, sequencing, and loop programming in Arduino. It also shows how multiple LEDs can be coordinated to produce creative lighting effects commonly used in decorative displays and electronic projects.
